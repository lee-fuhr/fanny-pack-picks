Fanny Pack Questions, Answered

Updated June 2026Independently researchedNo paid placement.

Quick answer

A fanny pack – also called a belt bag, waist pack, or hip bag – is a small hands-free carryall worn around your waist, hips, or chest. Modern designs have shed their 80s reputation and now come in sleek, technical fabrics with enough room for phone, wallet, keys, and a lip balm, making them ideal for travel, festivals, hiking, or daily errands when you want to travel light.

What size should a fanny pack be?

Most everyday fanny packs fall into two volume categories: slim or compact (roughly 1–2 liters) and standard (2–4 liters). A slim pack holds just your phone, cards, and keys with minimal bulk, while a standard size can also fit a small power bank, sunglasses, or a travel-sized sunscreen. Choose based on what you plan to carry daily. If you only need the essentials, a smaller pack keeps things streamlined. If you want to stash a light jacket or a full-size water bottle, look for a larger belt bag with compression straps. Remember that a pack that is too full will bulge and become uncomfortable, so aim for a size that leaves a little breathing room.

How do I wear a fanny pack? Around the waist or across the chest?

It’s entirely personal, but both styles are widely used. Wearing it around your waist – with the pouch centered in front, on your hip, or slightly to the back – is the classic approach and works well for hiking or when you want quick access to your phone. Wearing it slung across your chest (often called a “sling” style) is popular for city travel and festivals because it keeps the bag in your line of sight and distributes weight more evenly. Try both and see what feels natural for your body type and activity. If you plan to run or bike, a waist fit that stays snug above your hip bones is usually best. For crowded spaces like a subway or concert, cross-body gives you extra security and faster access.

What materials should I look for?

Durability and weather resistance are key. Most well-regarded fanny packs use nylon, polyester, or ripstop fabric with a water-repellent coating. For all-weather protection, look for packs with sealed zippers or a built-in rain fly. Canvas or waxed cotton options are stylish but heavier and not as water-resistant. The strap material matters too: a wide, padded belt (about 1.5 to 2 inches) is much more comfortable when carrying a full load than a thin nylon strap. Look for a buckle that is easy to operate one-handed, especially if you’ll be taking the pack on and off frequently.

Are fanny packs secure against pickpockets?

A fanny pack is far more secure than a back pocket or an open tote, but it’s not pickproof. Worn in front or cross-body, you can keep a hand on the pouch and feel any attempt to open it. Look for packs with anti-theft features like hidden zipper pulls, slash-resistant fabric, or a locking buckle. Many popular models from brands like Pacsafe or Travelon are designed specifically with security in mind. That said, the biggest security advantage is that the bag stays on your body. You are unlikely to leave it on a café chair or have it snatched off a shoulder. Still, practice common sense: keep valuables in a zippered compartment and avoid overstuffing the main pocket so the zipper stays fully closed.

Can I use a fanny pack for hiking or running?

Absolutely, but choose a pack designed for movement. Running-specific belt bags are usually ultra-lightweight, have bounce-free straps with a silicone grip lining, and hold just a phone and a key. Hiking versions are larger, often with a bottle holder and multiple pockets for snacks, map, and a small first aid kit. Look for packs that sit snugly without riding up. Some have adjustable stabilizer straps that clip around the hip belt to keep the pack from shifting during high-impact activity. Water resistance is also more important for outdoor use; a splash-proof pack will protect your phone from rain or sweat.

How do I clean and care for a fanny pack?

Most nylon or polyester packs can be hand-washed with mild soap and cold water, then air-dried. Avoid machine washing or machine drying, which can damage the coating and shrink the strap. For canvas or leather packs, spot-clean with a damp cloth and a gentle cleaner specified for the material. Always check the care tag. If the pack has a removable liner or foam padding, take it out before cleaning. Zippers should be kept free of grit – a quick wipe with a dry cloth after a dusty hike will keep them gliding smoothly.

Frequently asked questions

Are fanny packs unisex?

Yes, absolutely. Most modern belt bags are designed in neutral colors and shapes suitable for any gender. Some brands now market their packs as “unisex” or simply as “waist packs,” moving away from gendered labeling.

Can I fit a tablet or a Kindle in a fanny pack?

A small Kindle (6-inch screen) will fit in a standard 3-liter belt bag, but a full-size iPad or a Kindle Paperwhite with a case will not. If you need to carry a tablet, look for a “fly fishing” style hip pack, which is larger (4–6 liters) and has space for a slim device.

Will a fanny pack work for airport travel?

Yes, it’s a great choice for keeping your passport, boarding pass, phone, and wallet together and accessible. Just be aware that TSA may still ask you to remove it if the metal buckle sets off a detector – it’s better to wear it through security and place it in the bin if needed.

How do I know if a fanny pack will fit my waist or hips?

Most packs come with an adjustable strap that fits a range of 25 to 55 inches. Check the product description: many now list a specific range. If you have a larger waist or plan to wear the pack over bulky clothing, look for models with an extended strap or an extender sold separately.

What’s the difference between a fanny pack and a bum bag?

None, they are the same thing. “Fanny pack” is the common term in North America, while “bum bag” is used in the UK, Australia, and New Zealand. Other names include “waist bag,” “hip pack,” and “belt bag.”

Can I wear a fanny pack over a coat or backpack?

Yes. For winter wear, simply loosen the strap enough to fit around a puffy jacket or parka. It will still sit on your hips. If you are also wearing a backpack, you can clip the fanny pack in front or let it ride on your hip under the backpack hip belt – just be careful not to block the backpack’s waist strap adjusters.

In shortThe beauty of a fanny pack is that it lets you move through your day without constantly managing a bag, pocket, or purse. Focus on fit, material, and the way you actually carry your essentials, and you’ll find a style that feels like second nature. Whether you are navigating a city, hiking a trail, or just running errands, a well-chosen belt bag is the kind of gear that quietly makes everything easier.
